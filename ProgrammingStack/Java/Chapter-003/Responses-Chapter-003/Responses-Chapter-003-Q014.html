Floating point numbers can be represented in two ways in memory:-

1. Single Precision Representation:-

This is 32 bit representation of the floating point number. The format for single precision representation is:-

Sign Bit = 1 bit (sign bit 0 means that the number is +ve where as 1 means that the number is -ve)
Exponent Bits = 8 bits (this is used to represent the power of 2 in the binary representation of the floating point number)
Mantissa Bits = 23 bits (this is used to represent the part of the floating point number in the binary representation which comes after the decimal point)

Example,
Lets consider a number, 1460.125

For single precision representation or 32 bit representation, we first convert the number to binary

1460.125 (decimal) = 1 011 011 01 00 . 001 (binary)

Then we normalize the binary representation as,
1 . 011 011 01 00 001 X (2^10)

Now, in this normalized representation,

Mantissa (the part after decimal) = 011 011 01 00 001
Exponent (the power of 2) = 10

The format for representing the number in single precision is:-

[S](1 bit) [E+127](8 bits) [M](23 bits)

The sign bit in this case would be 0 since the number is +ve.
E+127 = 10+127 = 137 (decimal) = 1 000 1 001 (binary)
M = 011 011 01 00 001 (to complete the remaining bits 0's are added towards the end of the binary representation). Thus, M = 011 011 01 00 001 000 000 000 000 000 0000

Final Representation,
0 10001001 01101101000010000000000000000000

2. Double Precision Representation

This is 64 bit representation of the floating point number. The format for double precision representation is:-

Sign Bit = 1 bit (sign bit 0 means that the number is +ve where as 1 means that the number is -ve)
Exponent Bits = 11 bits (this is used to represent the power of 2 in the binary representation of the floating point number)
Mantissa Bits = 52 bits (this is used to represent the part of the floating point number in the binary representation which comes after the decimal point)

Example,
Lets consider a number, 1460.125

For double precision representation or 64 bit representation, we first convert the number to binary

1460.125 (decimal) = 1 011 011 01 00 . 001 (binary)

Then we normalize the binary representation as,
1 . 011 011 01 00 001 X (2^10)

Now, in this normalized representation,

Mantissa (the part after decimal) = 011 011 01 00 001
Exponent (the power of 2) = 10

The format for representing the number in double precision is:-

[S](1 bit) [E+1023](11 bits) [M](52 bits)

The sign bit in this case would be 0 since the number is +ve.
E+1023 = 10+1023 = 1033 (decimal) = 1 000 000 1001 (binary)
M = 011 011 01 00 001 (to complete the remaining bits 0's are added towards the end of the binary representation). Thus, M = 011 011 01 00 001 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000

Final Representation,
0 10000001001 0110110100001000000000000000000000000000000000000000
